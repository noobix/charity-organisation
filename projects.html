Water Project

Since 1997 we have had water projects for communities which live with clean water difficulties we have dag 62 wells,128 boreholes and 600 pipes. Extreme poverty cannot end until clean water, toilets and hygiene are a normal part of daily life for everyone, everywhere. Yet 785 million people live without clean water close to home, and 2 billion don't have a decent toilet of their own. And climate change is making the situation worse. Women in particular waste precious time walking long distances to collect dirty water. Girls drop out of school because there are no private toilets to manage their periods. And approximately 289,000 children per year do not live to see their fifth birthday because of diarrhoeal diseases caused by dirty water and poor sanitation. We may be called WaterAid, but our focus is resolutely on all three essentials: clean water, improved sanitation and proper hygiene.

Clothing Project

Since 1992 we have taken donation in clothing and redistributed to needy communities. The clothing project has secured a 2-acre land for the construction of a school for the children of Dwen Daama deprived rural community in the central region of Ghana which has no school. Our clothing project School shall provide a tuition-free education with free uniforms, free stationary, free lunch and all other needed things for the successful education of our beneficiaries. The purpose of this project is to make education accessible to the children of Dwen Daama and its surrounding villages, and boost motivation for education among the children and parents of the community, in pursuance of our mission to empower the less-privileged people of Ghana for a better future. We are counting on the support of kindhearted individuals like you to make this project a success. Your donation of US$ 6 would buy us one bag of cement for the construction of the school. Please donate today!

Medical Mission

Since 2012 we have purschased non-prescription drugs and these OTC drugs have been supplied to needy people for first aid treatment. It started as a group of dedicated pharmacy students going on a pharmacy medical mission. Our patient clientele were poor impoverished patients of both rural and urban cities in Ghana. We struggled to obtain medications for the mission and shopped differrent retail pharmacies for vitamins, supplements, and analgesics. We would purhcase those on sale and clearance but often times we paid full prices for these medications. There was limited funding for the mission and most everything came out of pocket from team members, friends, and families. In 2009, we purchased 2 luggages worth of medications and treated approximately 1,000 patients within a 3 week mission. The medication list includes vitamins, supplements and OTC analgesics. In 2016, we brought back ~ 14 luggages filled with medications such as: vitamins, supplements, OTC analgesics, injectable lidocain, injectable anti-inflammatories, hypertension, oral diabetes melitus, antifungal creams, and so much more. The team successfuly treated more than 2,500 patients in 2016. We understand that struggle for small non profits trying to help patients but doesnt have the means. Medical mission main focus is access to medications for eligible non profit organizations providing healthcare to indigent patients. For more information please visit our Medication Assistance Program “MAP” tab on the main page.
